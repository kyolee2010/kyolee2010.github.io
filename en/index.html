1. Manipulation of Exotic Fields with Acoustic Functional Materials

This direction focuses on the fundamental mechanisms of acoustic field manipulation in artificial structures and functional materials, exploring wave propagation, interference, and localization in complex media. The research emphasizes resonant coupling, wavefront shaping, and nonreciprocal sound transmission to elucidate the physical nature of soundâ€“matter interactions, providing theoretical foundations and design principles for precise acoustic energy control and advanced acoustic devices.

2. Application Explorations of Acoustic Functional Materials

Aimed at the noise control and acoustic optimization needs of major equipment, this research advances the applied fundamentals of acoustic functional materials. It develops high-performance sound-absorbing metamaterials and multifunctional composites based on deep-subwavelength structural design, and systematically investigates energy management mechanisms involving resonance, coupling, interference, and dissipation. The goal is to realize broadband, efficient, lightweight, and programmable sound absorption and insulation schemes that support innovation in transportation, aerospace, and precision acoustic engineering.

3. Non-Hermitian and Topological Acoustics

This research area explores the emerging intersection of non-Hermitian physics and topological acoustics, focusing on the acoustic realization and dynamics of gain/loss modulation, exceptional points (EPs), and topological edge states. It uncovers new physical phenomena such as nonreciprocal sound propagation, asymmetric mode coupling, and robust energy transport. By constructing non-Hermitian topological structures and nonreciprocal acoustic channels, the work advances robust acoustic communication, reconfigurable wave control, and intelligent acoustic systems.
